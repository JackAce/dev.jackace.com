In the video, Alex asked me what the probability is for losing the two double street bets 4 times in a row (essentially busting out). Here's the calculation:

\begin{align} P_{4\,losses\,in\,a\,row} & = \frac{14}{38}^4 \\ \\ & \approx {0.36842}^4 \\ \\ & \approx 0.0184237 \\ \\ & \approx 1.84237% \\ \end{align}

Which means that you'd lose 4 in a row once every 54.3 attempts. For every attempt, you'll either win $5 or you will lose $400.

For Single-Zero, the probability of losing four bets in a row is...

\begin{align} P_{4\,losses\,in\,a\,row} & = \frac{13}{37}^4 \\ \\ & \approx {0.351351}^4 \\ \\ & \approx 0.0152394 \\ \\ & \approx 1.52394% \\ \end{align}

Which means that you'll lose 4 in a row once every 65.6 attempts.

I'm not sure why the system author made $810 the buy in. It seems like if you wanted to bet another tier after betting $270, the next tier would be $810, but the way this system stands, you don't have enough to make this bet. You'd bet better off buying in for $400 or for $1210 depending on whether or not you wanted to try a 5th tier.
