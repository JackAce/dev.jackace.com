What is an Outcome Graph and how does it show you what to expect from a Roulette betting pattern?

Let's see the Graph!

There's a new feature to the Roulette Bet Analyzer. I've added something that I call Outcome Graphs.

When you enter the details for your Roulette system, a graph will show up at the bottom of the tool.

Betting Pattern 1: $350 Straight-up Bet

Here's what the Outcome Graph looks like for a single $350 bet on the number {% include roulette-spot.html number="21" -%}.

The graph is a running sum of all the possible outcomes sorted by the worst loss to the highest win.

Here, we can see we will lose 37 times, and win back $350x35 = {% include win-loss-amount.html amount=12250 %} on the 38th result.

And after 38 trials and $13,300 in wagers, we'd be down {% include win-loss-amount.html amount=-700 %} as expected. And this makes sense, because $700/$13,300 is 5.26%, which is the house edge in Double-Zero Roulette.

Betting Pattern 2: $10 on 10 Straight-up Bets

On the other side of the spectrum, here's a different betting pattern, where we bet $10 on the numbers {% include roulette-spot.html number="1" %} through {% include roulette-spot.html number="35" -%}.

And here's the Outcome Graph for this system:

Here, you can see 3 sharp {% include win-loss-amount.html amount=-350 %} drops followed by 35 small upward {% include win-loss-amount.html amount=10 %} bumps.

So, here you can see how these very different systems generate very different graphs.

The first system looks like a reverse checkmark, where there's a steep climb on the right. This corresponds to a system with lots of (relatively small) losses and one giant jackpot at the end.

The second system looks like a checkmark, with a sharp drop on the left. This corresponds to a few whacks, with many, many small wins that inch you back to the expected loss.

This pattern is more balanced than the first 2 systems and the graph reflects this.
