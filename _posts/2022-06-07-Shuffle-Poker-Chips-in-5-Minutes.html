If you play live poker, you must know how to shuffle poker chips. If you don't, can you even claim to be a real poker player?

Brad Owen shuffles his chips.
Rampage shuffles his chips.
Even Poker Bunny shuffles her chips.

So if you want to be taken seriously at the live poker table, you have to learn, too.

What do you need to Shuffle Poker Chips?

To learn how to shuffle poker chips, you need three things:

  • The proper materials
  • Preparation
  • Proper technique

Most of the tutorials I've seen only talk about technique. That's usually fine if you're planning on practicing at a casino, but most people will be learning at home.

Materials

There are two material items you need to shuffle poker chips:

  • Quality Poker Chips
  • A Padded Surface

Quality Poker Chips

This is the obvious item, but it helps if you use chips that have a bit of wear and tear to them. It really helps. A rounded edge makes chip shuffling a lot easier than with a squared edge.

Metal Dollar Tokens

If you can get your hands on 8 of the old-school Vegas slot tokens, that is the absolute easiest type of chip you can start with. They are usually well weathered and are easy to shuffle. You basically just need to push them together and they'll shuffle themselves. It is a great way to get the feel of how the chips should be coming together and interleaving.

Slick Plastic Casino Chips

These older style chips are somewhat difficult to shuffle for the beginner. The chips tend to stick together.

Composite Clay/Plastic Chips

These are the most popular style of casino chip and probably the easiest to shuffle (besides the metal tokens). They become a lot easier to shuffle if the edges are worn.

Standard "Home Game" Poker Chips

And the poker chips commonly used in "home games" and available for relatively cheap on Amazon work well, too. Just make sure you break them in using techniques in the preparation part of this post.

These work well, but they're not as heavy as casino chips. They don't have the same feel to the, but you shouldn't have a problem learning with these style chips.

A Padded Surface

It's rare to see a chip shuffling tutorial talk about your practice surface. A lot of shitty videos I've seen just use a hard kitchen table.

A hard kitchen table is a horrible surface for learning chip shuffling.

You should try using one of the following surfaces to learn this skill (in order by highest preference):

  • A casino table/poker table
  • A padded surface
  • A thin towel or blanket over a hard table

If you're not near a poker table, I've found the next best thing is to use a padded dish drying mat. I found a great one at Amazon for under $11.

Preparation

There are two main steps in preparing to shuffle chips.

  • Wash the chips
  • Wash your hands

These are two often overlooked steps in learning chip shuffling. Any oil in your hands is going to make learning a lot more difficult. Also, new poker chips will often have a fine powder on them, which makes precise handling of the chips hard.

So wash your hands well before you start practicing. I would also have some hand wipes to give your hands a quick rinse if you build up any oil in your hands while practicing. Trust me, this is going to help you a lot more than you might realize.

And wipe the chips down, as well. Old casino chips will often have oil on them from being man-handled for so long.

Technique

You should learn how to shuffle chips in the following order:

  • Shuffle 2+2 Metal Dollar Slugs (if available)
  • Shuffle 4+4 Metal Dollar Slugs (if available)
  • Shuffle 2+2 Poker Chips
  • Shuffle 4+4 Poker Chips
  • Shuffle 8+8 Poker Chips
  • Shuffle 4+4 Chips (Hard Mode)
  • Shuffle 8+8 Chips (Hard Mode)
  • BONUS: Repeat all these steps for your non-dominant hand (I did for this post!)

Shuffling Metal Dollar Slugs

If you can get a hold of some old school metal dollar slugs that casinos used to use in slot machines, they are excellent for getting a feel for the shuffle.

The edges are usually well-worn and you can basically just squeeze the tokens together and they will magically interleave.
